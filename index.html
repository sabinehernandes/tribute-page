Annie Easley

Here's a timeline of Annie Easley's life

  • 1933 - Born in Birmingham, Alabama
  • 1952 - Earned a degree in Pharmacy from Xavier University in New Orleans, Louisiana
  • 1955 - Began her career as a “human computer,” doing computations for researchers, analyzing problems and doing calculations by hand. When hired, she was one of only four African-American employees at the Lab. Even in the face of discrimination, she persevered
  • 1960s - 1970s - Developed and implemented computer code that analyzed alternative power technologies, supported the Centaur high-energy upper rocket stage, that heped determine alternative systems to solve energy problems
  • 1970s - Worked on a project examining damage to the ozone layer and with massive cuts in the NASA space program, began working on energy problems such as determining the life use of storage batteries and economic advantages of co-generating power plants that obtained byproducts from coal and steam
  • 1980s - Became a Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) counselor, helping her supervisors at NASA address discrimination complaints from all levels
  • 1977 - Obtains a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from Cleveland State University
  • 1989 - After retiring, she remained an active participant in the Speaker's Bureau and the Business & Professional Women's association
  • 2011 - Passed away in Cleveland, Ohio, leaving behind a legacy of pioneering contributions to space exploration and science

“I just have my own attitude. I'm out here to get the job done, and I knew I had the ability to do it, and that's where my focus was.”

- Annie Easley
